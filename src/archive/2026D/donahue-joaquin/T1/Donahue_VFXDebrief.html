Real Time VFXs for Games and Interactive Media Debrief on my work in Unreal Engine’s Niagara System

Introduction

Before this class I had no experience with VFX work and Unreal Engine’s Niagara System, meaning I had to learn the basics. My goal for this class was to be able to make a VFX piece of my own without having to follow a tutorial. I believe I learned enough to be able to achieve this for my future projects. Here’s some beginner tutorials I followed at the start of the term before diving into my VFX work:

  • https://youtu.be/hnUQiwJweeg?si=lUP6GOT_w_WOtaNP (Beginner Niagara Introduction)
  • https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UTO5ohy8-7M&list=PLoKWe7r9HkWzRW6H3hkZSlBuw0m_ZwFNN&index=2 (Beginner Niagara Introduction)
  • https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oQ03crNjZ_I&list=PLoKWe7r9HkWzRW6H3hkZSlBuw0m_ZwFNN&index=11 (Basic Material Node Knowledge)

Sword Slash VFX

This was the first project I worked on after studying the basics of the Niagara system. I followed this tutorial: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=djlnnPvFR0Q&list=PLoKWe7r9HkWzRW6H3hkZSlBuw0m_ZwFNN&index=4 

It stands out and achieves its look by layering multiple effects within one Niagara System. When I followed it for the first time, I struggled to keep up with the Youtuber’s fast pace and by the end of the video I realized I mindlessly followed along without registering what I was actually doing. Rewatching it and taking pauses to understand what each node and setting he adjusted did, was more helpful in comprehending the systems.

Stylized Waterfall VFX

This was the second project I worked on. I followed this tutorial: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=graozMcShMA&list=PLoKWe7r9HkWzRW6H3hkZSlBuw0m_ZwFNN&index=8

This effect also layers multiple effects within one Niagara System. This tutorial really helped me understand how much you can do with a single texture. The foam, white spots on the waterfall and the different types of water ripples all use the same texture. This was also the first VFX I made that emitted different meshes. It truly helped me understand how flexible Niagara and Unreal Engine’s materials can be.

Sci-fi Barrier VFX

This was the my last project I worked on. I followed this tutorial: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=omkwqdWMB_U&list=PLoKWe7r9HkWzRW6H3hkZSlBuw0m_ZwFNN&index=13

Even if this tutorial seemed less ambitious, it covered topics the other two videos didn’t teach me. This video teached me 2 different ways to make my own textures, one using Adobe Photoshop after exporting the UVs from Maya and the other using a software called Material Maker. Apart from that, it taught me how to make a quick in-engine animation to summon a mesh in Niagara.

Conclusion

After working on all these projects and doing tons of research on topics they covered I didn’t fully grasp at first, I feel ready to attempt making my own VFX. Here’s an in-engine  video with my blueprints and my Niagara Systems: https://youtu.be/LAlkqHjinio
